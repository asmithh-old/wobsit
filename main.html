Projects

Neural network word embeddings for Harry Potter fanfiction My final project for 6.806, Advanced Natural Language Processing. I tried to see if slash fanfiction used language in a distinctly unique way, as measured in learned word embeddings using Google's Word2Vec.

Analysis of link behavior in the Gamergate controversy: A project with Nate Matias that was a continuation of work I did during my summer 2015 internship at the Berkman Center.

Random walks based on keystroke timing noise : my first project in Javascript! Uses the last few decimal places of keystroke timing as a source of randomness to draw a Levy flight. The high-precision timing module doesn't work consistently well in Chrome.

January 2015 Internship at Open Technology Institute: In which I attempt to figure out what demographic factors are correlated with good broadband access and try and fail to scrape telecom providers' websites.

"Knitting the Cozy Sweater of Identity": My CMS.400 (Credit for Reddit) final paper (a team effort with Allan K. and Aaron S.), featuring a physical data visualization, figuring out how to understand how people think, and some not-so-well-hidden existential dread. Also featured in a MIT admission blog post by Allan.

that weird Twitter bot I made once: This is the source code I threw together for a Twitter bot that combines the infamous Harry Potter fanfiction My Immortal, an extract from A Brief History of Time by Stephen Hawking, and another excerpt from Faulkner's The Sound and the Fury . On Twitter here

Markov-chain-generated Rush lyrics: Using a corpus of the Canadian band Rush's lyrics to generate bigram probabilities, I wrote a script to make new Rush songs. There's a Flask web app for it somewhere, but I have no idea where.

Simulation of tidal bulges in the event of an improbable apocalypse: Project for introductory physics done in Processing -- an animation of the Earth's tidal bulges if the moon were to (near-impossibly) come twice as close to Earth at perigee.
